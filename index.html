About

An AI that remembers you between chats.

Mr. Mags is a persistent memory layer for AI. Tell it once — your projects, your style, your clients, your students, the brief — and every new conversation starts where the last one left off. No more re-explaining yourself every time you open a fresh chat.

It pairs with Claude Desktop today. ChatGPT, Gemini, and the rest light up through the browser extension as it ships. One memory, every AI you use.

Everything you tell Mr. Mags lives on your machine. Nothing leaves it. There is no account, no cloud, no server-side database with your name on it. The promise isn't "we won't read your data" — it's that we can't, because we don't have it.

Who it's for

Anyone tired of re-explaining themselves.

Lawyers walking AI through a case. Therapists onboarding a client. Founders catching new hires up. Writers building a world. Engineers carrying a codebase. Researchers tracking a thread. Teachers planning a year. Anyone who has opened a new chat and thought "ugh, here we go again."

Named for a school teacher whose students call him Mr. Mags. He was the first user — his lesson plans, rubrics, and parent emails kept evaporating into one-shot chats that forgot him the moment he opened a new conversation. Now they don’t. The shape worked for him; turns out it works for everyone whose work has context worth keeping.
